Sittham (चित्त · சித்தம்) is the consciousness layer — it reads your raw knowledge and writes it into Sakthi's nine role wings as a local-first knowledge graph.
Local-first. ChromaDB-backed. ~300 MB embedding model. Runs on your machine. Cloud LLMs see only the snippets retrieved per query — never the whole memory.
MIT-licensed. Forkable. Auditable. Your Sakthi belongs to you. Bootstrap once: uv tool install sakthi-graph && sakthi init --sabha ~/sakthi

The Problem
Most AI replies are option-shaped. Operators need the move.
When you're running something, "here are five ways to think about it" is exhausting. You need a recommendation, a tradeoff, the next action — not a survey of possibilities.
The generic reply
Option A: reduce headcount Option B: cut SaaS spend Option C: defer payroll Option D: raise a bridge Consider your risk tolerance and...
Sabha says
Routing: CFO. Cut SaaS 40%. You lose the analytics tier. Worth it — that tier isn't driving renewals.
Sample Reply — verbatim Sabha output
Routing: CFO.

Cut the SaaS line 40%.

You lose the analytics tier.

Worth it because that tier
isn't driving renewals.
Routing: CFO. role declared first
You lose the analytics tier. tradeoff named
Worth it because… reason given
Five Disciplines — Applied on Every Load-Bearing Reply
01
A Role
Every question routed to a C-suite function. Declared at the top of the reply, always.
02
A Recommendation
The role recommends. Doesn't survey. "Do X" beats a five-option list every time.
03
A Tradeoff
"Do X. You lose Y. Worth it because Z." Names what's given up. Decisiveness and honesty together.
04
Mode Discipline
ask for chat replies. engage for filed documents. Explicit, not implicit. Never conflated.
05
Local Memory
Your Sakthi compounds on your machine. Every engage session writes a diary entry. The council learns your world.
